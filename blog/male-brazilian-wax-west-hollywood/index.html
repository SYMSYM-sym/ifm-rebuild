Male Brazilian Wax in West Hollywood: What the Appointment Covers

6 min read The IFM Team

A male Brazilian wax in West Hollywood removes all hair from the pubic region, including the shaft, scrotum, and the area between the buttocks. The appointment takes 30 to 45 minutes in a private room. You undress from the waist down, lie on a treatment table, and the practitioner applies hard wax in sections. The result lasts two to four weeks.

What the service includes

The male Brazilian covers five zones: the pubic mound, the shaft, the scrotum, the perineum, and the area between the buttocks. Hair is removed from the root using hard wax, which adheres to the hair shaft without sticking to the skin. The practitioner works in small sections, applying wax, letting it set for a few seconds, then pulling it quickly in the opposite direction of growth.

You lie on your back for the front and sides, then turn to your stomach or pull your knees toward your chest for the back area. The practitioner adjusts your position as needed and uses one hand to hold the skin taut while removing the wax with the other. This tension reduces discomfort and prevents bruising.

Most men leave the salon smooth. If you prefer a small patch of hair on the pubic mound, you can request that before the appointment begins. Otherwise, the default is full removal.

What happens during the appointment

You arrive at the salon on Larrabee Street in West Hollywood and check in. The practitioner brings you to a private room with a treatment table, a small cart with supplies, and a towel. You undress from the waist down and lie on the table. A disposable drape covers your midsection until the practitioner begins.

The first section is the pubic mound. The practitioner applies a thin layer of wax, waits a few seconds for it to harden, then pulls it off. You feel a sharp pull that lasts less than a second. The practitioner moves quickly through the area, working in strips about two inches wide.

Next is the shaft. The practitioner holds the skin taut and applies wax in small patches. The wax grips the hair but does not stick to the skin. The scrotum requires more tension and smaller sections because the skin is thinner. You may be asked to hold the skin in place while the practitioner works.

The final area is between the buttocks. You turn to your stomach or pull your knees up. The practitioner applies wax along the crease and removes it in quick pulls. The entire process takes 30 to 45 minutes depending on hair density and whether this is your first appointment.

Pain level and what to expect

The first Brazilian is the most uncomfortable because the hair is thicker and the follicles are stronger. Each pull feels sharp for a fraction of a second, then fades. The scrotum and the area between the buttocks are more sensitive than the pubic mound or shaft. Most men describe the sensation as intense but manageable.

Pain decreases with each appointment. Hair grows back finer after repeated waxing, and the follicles weaken. By the third or fourth visit, most men report the discomfort is half of what it was initially.

You can take ibuprofen 30 minutes before the appointment to reduce inflammation. Avoid caffeine and alcohol the day of your wax—both tighten the skin and make hair removal more uncomfortable. Arrive with clean, dry skin and at least a quarter inch of growth. Shorter hair does not grip the wax well, and longer hair can break instead of pulling cleanly from the root.

"Men expect the Brazilian to be worse than it is. The anticipation is usually harder than the actual appointment. After the first few strips, most clients settle in and realize they can handle it." — Rene', practitioner

How long results last

A male Brazilian wax lasts two to four weeks depending on your hair growth cycle and how consistently you wax. Hair grows in three phases: anagen (active growth), catagen (transition), and telogen (rest). Waxing removes hair in all three phases, but only hair in the anagen phase will take longer to return.

The first week after your appointment, your skin stays completely smooth. By week two, you may notice fine regrowth in isolated patches. By week three, most men see enough regrowth to book their next appointment. Consistent waxing every four to six weeks trains the hair to grow in the same cycle, which makes future results last longer.

Exfoliate gently starting three days after your wax. Use a dry brush or a chemical exfoliant with salicylic acid twice a week. This prevents ingrown hairs and keeps the skin smooth between appointments. Avoid tight clothing and heavy sweating for the first 24 hours to reduce irritation.

FAQ

Frequently asked

Do I need to do anything before my first male Brazilian wax in West Hollywood?

Grow your hair to at least a quarter inch, which is about two weeks of growth after shaving. Shower before your appointment and arrive with clean, dry skin. Avoid caffeine and alcohol the day of your wax. Wear loose cotton underwear and sweatpants or shorts to the salon.

How much does a male Brazilian wax cost at IFM?

The male Brazilian starts at $145. The price includes the full service—pubic mound, shaft, scrotum, and area between the buttocks. There are no hidden fees or add-ons. You pay the same rate whether this is your first visit or your tenth.

Can I get a Brazilian if I have sensitive skin?

Yes. Hard wax is gentler than soft wax and does not adhere to the skin. The practitioner applies a pre-wax oil to create a barrier between the wax and your skin, which reduces irritation. If you have a history of reactions, mention it when you book. The practitioner can test a small area before proceeding with the full service.

How soon can I work out or have sex after a Brazilian wax?

Wait 24 hours before heavy sweating or sexual activity. Your skin is open at the follicle level immediately after waxing, and friction or sweat can cause irritation or infection. After the first day, resume normal activity. Most men return to the gym and their regular routine without issue.
