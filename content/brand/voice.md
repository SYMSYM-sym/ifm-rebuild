# IFM Brand Voice

We sound like a quiet, expert practitioner — confident, plain-spoken, never hyped.

## Tone
- Luxury-restrained × warm-professional. Think Aesop's journal × Hims blog.
- Adult. Clear. Direct. Never coy, never euphemistic, never crude.
- Discretion is a value. Talk about intimate topics matter-of-factly, the same way a doctor would.

## Sentence rules
- Short sentences win. Mix in a longer one occasionally for rhythm.
- One idea per sentence. Cut adverbs.
- Active voice over passive ("Rene' shapes the wax" not "The wax is shaped by Rene'").
- Concrete over abstract ("a 7-minute drive from Beverly Hills" not "conveniently located").

## Word choices
- "Wax" not "treatment" (when the service is wax).
- "Appointment" not "session."
- "Practitioner" or "esthetician" not "technician."
- "Skin" not "epidermis."
- We say "men" — we don't say "guys" or "dudes."
- "Discreet" and "private" are signature words; use them where natural, not every paragraph.

## Things we NEVER do
- Emojis.
- Exclamation points beyond the very rare emphatic ending.
- Superlatives without evidence ("best", "most", "ultimate").
- Medical claims ("cures", "heals", "treats").
- Promises about pain-free experiences.
- Coy euphemisms for genitals or bodily functions. Use plain anatomical terms or service names.
- Stock-tone openings ("In today's fast-paced world…", "Let's dive in…").
- "Game-changer", "elevate", "revolutionize", "synergy", "next-level", "level up", "tap into", "leverage".
- Listicles with rhyming headers ("Get Smooth, Stay Smooth!").

## Structure rules for every article
- Open with a 40–60 word answer paragraph that directly responds to the article's question/keyword. AI engines extract this verbatim.
- One H1 (the article title). 3–5 H2 sections. H3 only when needed.
- One quote-block per article in the practitioner's voice. Format:
  > "Direct quote from Rene', 1–3 sentences, specific and concrete."
- One FAQ block at the end with 4 Q&As, each Q a real long-tail query.
- Internal links: minimum 2 per article, anchored on natural phrases (no "click here").
- Location mentions: minimum 3 per article — neighborhood, cross street, or landmark.
- Word count: 900–1400. Hard floor 850, hard ceiling 1500.
- One factual stat at most. If we cite a number we cite a source. If we don't have a source, we don't cite the number.

## Author byline
Always "The IFM Team". Articles can quote Rene' but the byline does not change.

## Service references
When mentioning prices, link to /#services and use the live numbers (do not invent prices). Current price band: Brazilian/Back/Chest/Arms/Eyebrows-equivalents range from $65+ to $195+; Full-Body Trimming is $150/hr. If you don't know a price, omit it — do not guess.
