Aftercare

Look after the results — the results look after you.

First 24 hours — don't

  • · Lie in the sun or a tanning bed
  • · Go swimming — no ocean, no chlorinated pools
  • · Use a jacuzzi, steam bath, or sauna
  • · Exercise or anything that makes you sweat

Your follicles are exposed to the elements and prone to infection. Give them a day.

Days 2–14 — exfoliate

Skin will begin to grow over the treated area, and pesky hairs will look for a way to break through. Help them along.

Exfoliate gently, in a soft circular motion, lightly scrubbing the surface of your skin a few times a week. This is the single most important thing you can do to prevent ingrowns.

Stay on schedule

We recommend rebooking every 4 to 6 weeks. The more consistent you are, the finer the regrowth — and the easier every future appointment becomes.

Frequently Asked

Everything you were going to ask.

What should I do to prepare for my appointment?

Come in freshly showered. Don't trim your hair — if any trimming is needed, Igor will take care of it before treatment. If you exercise regularly, schedule your appointment after your workout, not before.

What should I wear?

Dress comfortably. Avoid tight clothing — it can irritate freshly-waxed skin. Loose-fitting clothes are ideal, especially for intimate services.

How often should I wax?

Every 4 to 6 weeks. Some clients come in every 4 weeks, some every other month — the younger and finer the regrowth, the more comfortable every future treatment becomes, with less irritation afterward.

How much irritation is there?

Expect mild redness on treated areas. It typically fades within a couple of hours. Keep the area clean and disinfected and you'll see minimal to no lasting irritation.

Is it really private?

Yes — completely. IFM is a single-suite salon. You won't share a waiting room with anyone, and your appointment is one-on-one with Igor from start to finish.

First-time waxing — what should I expect?

A calm, non-judgmental environment and straightforward guidance. Igor has been doing this for 21+ years and works with first-timers constantly. You'll walk in nervous, walk out a regular.
